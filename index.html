Biography

Dr. Kate Patterson joined Central Wyoming College in 2014. Originally from Lander, Wyoming, she returned to the region after serving as a research fellow at the University of Auckland, New Zealand.

She holds a Ph.D. in Mathematics from Montana State University-Bozeman and a B.S. from the University of Wyoming. Her teaching philosophy centers on "shared curiosity," encouraging students to explore the elegance and complexity of mathematical modeling.

Kate is actively involved in collaborative scientific research, most notably participating in the Nationwide Eclipse Ballooning Project (NEBP). Working alongside Dr. Bill Finney and the CWC Ballooning Team, she contributes her mathematical expertise to atmospheric data collection and high-altitude scientific ballooning initiatives.
